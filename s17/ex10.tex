\subsection{Soit $A \in M_n(\mathbb{R})$ nilpotente telle que $A^T A = AA^T$. Montrer que $A = O_n$.}

\color{black}
\vspace{0.5cm}

Posons $B = AA^T$

\vspace{0.3cm}

$B$ est symétrique réelle donc diagonalisable.
\[ \exists P \in GL_n(\mathbb{R}), \quad P^{-1} B P = \begin{pmatrix} \lambda_1 & & \\ & \ddots & \\ & & \lambda_n \end{pmatrix} \]

\vspace{0.3cm}

$A$ nilpotente $\Rightarrow \exists q \in \mathbb{N}, \enspace A^q = 0$

\vspace{0.3cm}

$\Rightarrow B^q = (AA^T)^q = A^q (A^T)^q = 0 \quad$ car $A$ et $A^T$ commutent.

\vspace{0.3cm}

$\Rightarrow B$ nilpotente.

\vspace{0.3cm}

Donc $Sp(B) = \{0\}$ car $X^q$ poly annulateur.

\vspace{0.3cm}

Donc $B$ semblable à 0. \\
$B = 0$

\vspace{0.3cm}

$\forall X \in \mathbb{R}^n, \quad X^T A^T A X = X^T 0 X = 0$

\vspace{0.3cm}

$(AX)^T AX = \|AX\|^2 \Rightarrow AX = 0$

\vspace{0.3cm}

$\Rightarrow A = 0$

\vspace{0.5cm}

Donc \fbox{$A = O_n$}
